Climbing Gym Reviews

Here is the structured review corner for climbing gyms I have visited. Each review focuses on first impression, setting style, grading, facilities, atmosphere, location access, and small bonus details that make a gym memorable!

Review System

The same framework is used for each and every gym so reviews would stay comparable without becoming too technical. The goal is not to produce an “objective” ranking, but to describe what the gym actually feels like to climb in.

Core categories

  • Basic info
  • First impression
  • Setting style
  • Grading
  • Facilities
  • Atmosphere and community
  • Location and access
  • LGBTQ+ friendliness tag
  • Bonus and minus points
  • Final verdict

Rating categories

  • Overall experience: /5
  • Route Setting: /5
  • Space: /5
  • Atmosphere: /5
  • Staff/Service: /5
  • Facilities: /5

Facilities checklist

Facilities vary a lot by city and gym type. For Hong Kong gyms, I do not usually treat parking as a core category, but air conditioning matters, so it is included here.

Grading

Facilities

  • Rental shoes: Yes
  • Lockers: Not sure
  • Changing room: Yes
  • Washroom: Yes (it's usually really cold!)
  • Drinks: Available for a charge
  • Kilterboard: Yes
  • Moonboard: No
  • Air conditioning: Yes

Atmosphere and community

Comment on staff friendliness, crowd level, music, cleanliness, solo-climber comfort, and whether the gym felt welcoming to beginners.

Bonus points

  • +1 Free sanitary pads for the girlies to use!!! Very thoughtful.

Final verdict

Summarize who this gym is best for, who may not like it, and whether you would return.
